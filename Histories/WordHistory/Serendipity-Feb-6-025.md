Word: Serendipity
Meaning: The occurrence of finding something valuable or pleasant by chance.
Origin & Story:
Coined in 1754 by English writer Horace Walpole, inspired by the Persian fairy tale “The Three Princes of Serendip.” The tale features princes who made lucky discoveries accidentally but through cleverness. “Serendip” was an old name for Sri Lanka.
Usage: Finding that book in the library was pure serendipity!
