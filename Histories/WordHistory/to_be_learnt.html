1. Serendipity Meaning: Finding something valuable by chance. Origin: 1754, Horace Walpole, from Persian tale The Three Princes of Serendip (“Serend” = Sri Lanka). -- odne

2. Limerence Meaning: An intense, involuntary romantic attraction to someone. Origin: 1979, coined by psychologist Dorothy Tennov.

3. Petrichor Meaning: The smell of rain on dry earth. Origin: 1964, from Greek petra (stone) + ichor (fluid in gods’ veins).

4. Sonder Meaning: Realization that everyone has a complex life like yours. Origin: Coined by John Koenig in The Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows (2012).

5. Defenestration Meaning: The act of throwing someone out of a window. Origin: 1618, from Latin de (down) + fenestra (window). Famous in Bohemian history.

6. Ephemeral Meaning: Lasting a very short time. Origin: Greek ephemeros (“lasting only a day”).

7. Quixotic Meaning: Unrealistic, impractical, idealistic. Origin: From Cervantes’ novel Don Quixote (1605).

8. Incognito Meaning: Disguised; in a way that conceals identity. Origin: Latin incognitus (“unknown”).

9. Nemesis Meaning: An unbeatable rival or source of downfall. Origin: Greek goddess Nemesis, punisher of hubris.

10. Obfuscate Meaning: To make something unclear or confusing. Origin: Latin obfuscare (“to darken”).

11. Palimpsest Meaning: Something reused but still showing traces of the old. Origin: Greek palimpsestos, reused parchment in ancient writing.

12. Ubiquitous Meaning: Present everywhere at once. Origin: Latin ubique (“everywhere”).

13. Zenith Meaning: The highest point. Origin: Arabic samt ar-ras (“path above the head”).

14. Apocryphal Meaning: Of doubtful authenticity, though widely circulated. Origin: Greek apokryphos (“hidden, secret”).

15. Halcyon Meaning: Calm, peaceful, happy period. Origin: Greek myth: halcyon bird calms storms during nesting. 16. Euphoria Meaning: Intense happiness or excitement. Origin: Greek eu- (good) + pherein (to bear).

17. Cacophony Meaning: A harsh, unpleasant mix of sounds. Origin: Greek kakophonia (“bad sound”).

18. Lethologica Meaning: The inability to remember a specific word. Origin: Greek lethe (forgetfulness) + logos (word).

19. Zenithal Meaning: Related to the peak or highest point. Origin: Same root as zenith, Arabic samt.

20. Myriad Meaning: Countless or extremely great number. Origin: Greek myrias (“ten thousand”).

21. Susurrus Meaning: Whispering, rustling sound. Origin: Latin susurrare (“to whisper”).

22. Petrichor Meaning: (Bonus repeat) Smell of rain on dry earth. Unique sensory word.

23. Obsequious Meaning: Excessively obedient or attentive. Origin: Latin obsequiosus (“compliant, dutiful”).

24. Nefarious Meaning: Wicked or criminal. Origin: Latin nefarius (“wrong, wicked”).

25. Proclivity Meaning: Natural inclination or tendency. Origin: Latin proclivitas (“slope, predisposition”).

26. Raconteur Meaning: A person skilled at telling stories. Origin: French raconter (“to tell”).

27. Ineffable Meaning: Too great to be expressed in words. Origin: Latin ineffabilis (“unutterable”).

28. Epiphany Meaning: Sudden realization or insight. Origin: Greek epiphaneia (“manifestation, appearance”).

29. Zeitgeist Meaning: The defining spirit or mood of an era. Origin: German zeit (time) + geist (spirit).

30. Mellifluous Meaning: Sweet-sounding, pleasant to hear. Origin: Latin mellifluus (“flowing with honey”).
